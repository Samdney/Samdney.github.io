Already a longer time ago, I decided to discuss the issue 'How good is the education of pupils at school about infosec and computer science questions?' and 'How can we make it better?' In this part of my Blog I will have a look at this questions. Since myself be German and attended school in Bavaria, my focus will be mainly on schools in this places.

At first I wanted to start to tell you a little bit about my own experiences as pupil.

Computer science as school subject:
  • During my school time I had tree years computer science as school subjet. I was 13-16 years old (2001 - 2004) and I really liked it. In the first year I had a young women teacher who was enthusiastic for her work and infected me with this enthusiasm for computers. In year two and three I had a teacher which already said in the first lesson of school year: 'I'm only also a computer science teacher because I needed an addional subject for getting better my employment chance'. For me that time was nearly my first contact with computers at all and I enjoyed it every week again.
  • The technical infrastracture was ok for a school of this size and at this time. My school had several computer rooms for lessons. Since, as far as I know, only ONE TEACHER was responsible for the whole digital infrastructure, the most time there exists a lot of troubles with the computers. This poor man simply hadn't enough time to keep all up to date and working.
  • Content (no guarantee for completeness, it was a long time ago ;):
    • First year: Introduction in basic coding structures (if, for, while, ...) => Programming with Visual Basic! (Aaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!), Absolute and relative addressing in Excel (Comment: You are seeing this MS influence? => An important point for further discussion!)
    • Second and third year (I'm not able to remember exactly which content was in which year): Computer history stuff, How works a keyboard, a mouse, a monitor, a printer, ...?, Number systems (oct, hex, bin), Relational databases, Network topologies, Malware and 'computer security' (which meant: 'Don't click on this big button at this site which promised you a lot of money'), How works the Internet?, AND, OR, NOR, flip-flop, ..., Main hardware components of a computer and how they work together
